## What it is

Chamelo's only category-specific product: smart sport sunglasses built for golfers. Golf Shield is the product Chamelo points golfers to when they walk into Dick's, Golf Galaxy, or a pro shop and ask "what should I be wearing on the course?"

What "high-contrast" means in practice:
- Greens appear sharper and more saturated — easier to read break and slope
- White ball is more visible against fairway, rough, sky
- Reduces visual noise without darkening the whole scene
- Different from Shield's standard Smoke lens — same VLT range, different tuning

## Voice and positioning notes

- **Talk like a golfer talks**, not like a sport brand advertising to golfers. Reference real moments on the course: reading break, watching ball flight, the walk between holes, conditions changing on the front nine vs the back.
- Lead with the visibility benefit — greens pop, ball flight stands out, line stays clear. The tech (Eclipse™ tint control, half-rim, 54% to 17% VLT) is the why-it-works, not the headline.
- The half-rim downward view is the design hook that competitors don't have. Lean into it. "Frame stays out of your line of sight at address" is a real, tactile benefit.
- Don't oversell performance. We don't say "lower your score" or "play better." We say "see clearer," "stay focused," "no distractions." The customer connects the dots.
- Tone: confident, dry, golfer-knowing. Same register as Marbury's authenticity, just applied to a golfer audience. Less hype, more "this is the right tool."
- Avoid: generic sport clichés ("dominate the course," "elevate your game"), tech-spec dumps in hero copy, claims about handicap or scoring.

## Retail positioning notes (for Dick's, Golf Galaxy, pro shop briefs)

- Demo on display: the half-rim downward view differentiation. Set up a ball on a putting mat so customers can wear the glasses and look down. The "I can see the ball clearly" moment is the conversion.
- Compare-to-Oakley positioning: Oakley dominates golf eyewear with fixed-tint or photochromic options. Golf Shield gives manual control — slide darker for full sun, lighter for shade, same lens, same glasses, no swap.
- Price positioning: confirm against Oakley Prizm Golf range (~$200–$300) — Golf Shield needs to be priced to land in or just under that band
