## Universal writing rules

These apply to every product, every channel, every piece of copy.

### VLT (Visible Light Transmission) format
Always write the larger (clearer) number first: **"63% to 17%"** not "17% to 63%". Mirrors how the lens behaves for the customer — starts clear, gets darker.

### UV protection
Always write **"100% UVA/UVB protection."** Chamelo holds UV400 certification, which is the technical basis for the 100% claim. Never write "99.9%" in customer copy — that's a lab spec.

### Speed claims
Use **"0.1 seconds"** when accuracy matters. Use **"instant"** or **"instantly"** in hero copy.

### Audio language
- **"Open-ear audio"** or **"Bluetooth audio"** — never "bone conduction" (Chamelo is open-ear)
- Never call them "headphones"

### Charging cable
All new Chamelo products ship with USB-C. However, mixed inventory of older USB-A units is still in circulation. **Do not specify USB-A or USB-C in customer copy** until older inventory has sold through. Acceptable copy: "magnetic charging cable" without naming the wall-side connector.

### Customer count
- Brand-level: **"100,000+ customers"**
- The 50,000+ figure is Music Shield Gen 1 unit sales only — use it only in Music Shield context, never as a brand stat

### No bundles
Treat every product as an individual SKU. Never write "part of running bundle" or similar bundle framing. If a bundle promotion is active, it's a marketing offer, not part of the product identity.

### Pricing format
- Always reference both MSRP and current selling price when copy length allows: "$199 (MSRP $260)"
- Short headlines can use the current price alone
- The compare-at framing ($260 → $199) is more powerful when shown — use it wherever it fits

## Common spec mistakes to avoid

- VLT written smaller number first ("17% to 63%") — always larger first
- UV written as "99.9%" — always "100% UVA/UVB"
- Using the technical lens-tech names ("guest-host liquid crystal," "twisted nematic liquid crystal") in customer copy — use the trademarked names instead
- Specifying USB-A or USB-C in customer copy while mixed inventory is in market — use "magnetic charging cable"
- Conflating VLT ranges between products — Music Shield (63% to 17%) and Shield (54% to 17%) are different
- Dropping "Gen 2" from Music Shield consumer copy too early — we still use it while Gen 1 is in market
- Writing Calm and Energy as separate Aura products — they're lens choices on one frame
- Mixing Aura palette colors — Calm = Crystal/Indigo/Ruby/Violet, Energy = Clear/Yellow/Pink/Orange
- Claiming polarized lenses on Music Shield, Aura, Shield — only Dusk is polarized (Falcon has a built-in de-polarizer, different thing)
- Using "300 charge cycles" for Music Shield Gen 2 — that's Gen 1. Gen 2 is 500+
- Claiming audio on Shield, Aura (no audio), Zurix, Aura Rx, Aroza, Golf Shield, Falcon, or any tint-only product
- Writing brand customer count as 50,000+ — that's Music Shield Gen 1 unit sales. Brand count is 100,000+
- Saying "part of running bundle" or any bundle framing — every product is individual
- Saying "oversized" for Zurix — banned descriptor
- Calling Falcon "battery-powered" — Falcon is solar-cell powered (no batteries)
- Using "Italian acetate" framing for Zurix Glossy Black or White colorways — only the Tortoise variant is acetate; the others are TR90
- Treating Bomb Edition, La Melaza, or Domo as separate products — they're colorway editions of Shield and/or Music Shield, documented in their respective ambassador files
