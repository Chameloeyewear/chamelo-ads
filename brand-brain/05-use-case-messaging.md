## How to use this file

When briefing an ad, email, landing page, or retail pitch:

1. Find the audience the creative is targeting.
2. Read the friction section in their language — these are real customer phrases, not invented ones.
3. Pull the channel-specific hooks for the right product.
4. Check the "what not to do" notes — they include the specific anti-claims from 04 Section 8.
5. If you find yourself writing in brand-voice rather than customer-voice, stop. Go back to 04 and pull the verbatim phrase.

The hooks in this file are starting points and validated angles, not script. Always pressure-test against the friction language in the same section — if the hook doesn't speak directly to that friction, rewrite.

## Universal rules that apply to every audience and every channel

These come from the customer research. Violating them generates 1-star reviews regardless of who you're targeting.

### Use the customer's word: "instant"

65% of Amazon positives and 63% of Trustpilot positives use the word "instant" or "almost instant" to describe the tint change. **Do not write "fast," "rapid," or "lightning-quick" in our copy.** Use their word.

### Lead with motion on visual creative

Every paid creative shows the tint switching in motion. Every product landing page embeds a video demo above the fold. The single highest-leverage piece of creative in the lineup is a clear demonstration of the lens changing — static images of the product don't carry the proof.

### Aggressive risk reversal on every DTC landing page

47% of Shopify complaints and 70% of Trustpilot complaints are about shipping. The same product on Amazon has 0% shipping complaints. The DTC fulfillment is the rating problem, not the product. Every DTC landing page needs above-the-fold:
- Specific shipping commitment with a date (not "ships fast")
- Real CS commitment with channel and response time (not "we'll help")
- No-hassle return policy stated up front (not in fine print)

The aggressive version: "If your order doesn't ship by [date], we refund and you keep the glasses."

### Use Amazon's 4.0★ as the third-party proof point

The single cleanest external evidence the product itself is liked. Use it on the DTC site (with attribution), in PR, in wholesale conversations, in retail buyer pitches, in investor decks. Doesn't cost anything to deploy.

### Anti-claims that must not appear in any creative

These are listed in full in `04-consumer-language.md` Section 8. Quick reference:

- **No "polarized"** on Falcon copy — Falcon is not polarized
- **No "6.5 hour" battery claim** until QC is consistent or claim is down-spec'd
- **No "smart" framing** — use "adaptive" or "almost-smart" (customer phrase)
- **No "only you can hear" privacy claims** on audio — QC inconsistent
- **No "premium build" at $200+** until temple-arm structural fix ships across lineup
- **No "improve your game / lower your score"** outcome claims on Golf Shield
- **No medical/therapeutic claims** on light-sensitivity / migraine creative without legal review
- **No selling Falcon for fast-action ball sports** (baseball, basketball, tennis serves, MTB tree-shadow)

## Channels — how each one writes differently

### Paid Meta (Facebook + Instagram feed, reels)
- First 1-3 seconds is everything. Hook = visual + first 3 words together.
- Mobile-first. Sound off by default — visuals and on-screen text must carry.
- Tint motion happens in the first 3 seconds, not at the end.
- Avoid: text-heavy first frames, long preambles, opening with a question, leading with a feature spec.

### Organic Instagram (feed posts, reels, stories)
- Caption can be longer and more story-driven. First sentence still has to land.
- Voice more casual, personality-driven.
- Compliments-reaction format works well for Aura (the "are those changing color?" hook).

### Email
- Subject: 50 characters or less.
- Preview: 90 characters. Reinforces subject without repeating.
- Body: conversational. They opened it.
- CTA: action verb + outcome.

### Landing page
- H1: matches the ad headline that drove them here. Continuity is conversion.
- Sub-H1: supporting promise. Sets up hero CTA.
- Above-the-fold answers "is this what I clicked for?" in 3 seconds.
- Video demo embedded above the fold.

### Retail / pro shop / in-store
- One-liner a rep can actually say.
- Demo cue: what to put in the customer's hand.
- Comparison frame for the pitch (vs Oakley Prizm, vs Ray-Ban Meta, vs traditional acetate).
